# The Reasoning Revolution

> **Status: under construction.** Reading phase.

*Last updated: 2026-04-22.*

## Narrative job

Make the case that something qualitatively new happened between late 2024 and 2025 with reasoning-trained models, while fairly representing the live debate about whether that something is new capability or more efficient sampling from existing capability.

## Reading list

1. Wei et al. (2022), "Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in Large Language Models."
2. Kojima et al. (2022), "Large Language Models are Zero-Shot Reasoners." The "Let's think step by step" paper.
3. Wang et al. (2022), "Self-Consistency Improves Chain of Thought Reasoning."
4. Yao et al. (2023), "Tree of Thoughts."
5. OpenAI (2024), o1 system card and "Learning to Reason with LLMs" blog post.
6. OpenAI (2025), o3/o4-mini system card.
7. Shao et al. (2024), DeepSeekMath. The GRPO paper.
8. DeepSeek-AI (2025), DeepSeek-R1. The R1-Zero "aha moment."
9. Guo et al. (2025), DeepSeek-R1 in *Nature*.
10. Wen et al. (2025), CoT-Pass@K. The metric that answers "does RL expand the reasoning frontier?"
11. Kumar et al. (2024), "Training Language Models to Self-Correct via Reinforcement Learning."

## What to watch

Successors to o3 and R1 (expected throughout 2026). New results on test-time scaling laws. Work on the compute-optimal balance between train-time and test-time compute. Updates to the CoT-Pass@K methodology.
