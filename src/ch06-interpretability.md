# Seeing Inside: Mechanistic Interpretability

> **Status: under construction.** Reading phase.

*Last updated: 2026-04-22.*

## Narrative job

Turn the empirical case into a structural one. If we want to know whether the reasoning in Chapter 5 is "real," we look inside. Show what the tools can see, what they find, and what they cannot yet see.

## Reading list

1. Olah et al. (2020), "Zoom In: An Introduction to Circuits." Foundational framework.
2. Elhage et al. (2021), "A Mathematical Framework for Transformer Circuits." Attention heads as computational units. Induction heads.
3. Elhage et al. (2022), "Toy Models of Superposition." Why interpretability is hard.
4. Bricken et al. (2023), "Towards Monosemanticity." The SAE breakthrough.
5. Templeton et al. (2024), "Scaling Monosemanticity." Claude 3 Sonnet features (including Golden Gate Bridge).
6. Ameisen, Lindsey et al. (2025), "Circuit Tracing." The methodology.
7. Lindsey et al. (2025), "On the Biology of a Large Language Model." The findings: multi-hop, poetry planning, medical diagnosis.
8. Nanda et al. (2023), "Progress Measures for Grokking via Mechanistic Interpretability."

## What to watch

Automated interpretability methods. Scaling circuit analysis to larger models (Claude 3.5+, GPT-5-class). Adversarial interpretability: cases where circuits mislead. Attempts to push coverage above Anthropic's ~25% benchmark.
