# From Reasoning to Agency

> **This chapter is in progress.** What follows is the planned reading list and narrative outline. The finished prose will replace this scaffolding as the chapter is drafted. Track progress in the [changelog](changelog.md).

*Last updated: 2026-04-22.*

## Narrative job

Show the transition from a system that generates text to a system that takes action. Give a general reader enough to understand why this is a regime change, not a UX change. End on where the frontier actually is: long-horizon tasks under economic pressure.

## Reading list

1. Yao et al. (2022), ReAct. The pattern.
2. Shinn et al. (2023), Reflexion. Self-improvement via reflection.
3. Anthropic (2024), Model Context Protocol announcement and specification.
4. Google (2025), Agent-to-Agent Protocol announcement and specification.
5. Zechner (2025), "Build Your Own Minimal Coding Agent."
6. The OpenClaw README and associated release post.
7. METR (2025), task horizon report. Doubling-every-seven-months methodology.
8. HKUDS (2026), ClawWork. Economic-pressure evaluation.
9. Agentic AI survey, arXiv:2510.25445. Taxonomy and the model-native paradigm.
10. Jimenez et al. (2024), SWE-bench.

## What to watch

Long-horizon task completion benchmarks beyond METR. New agent protocols or standards beyond MCP/A2A. Production deployment data from agentic systems. The economic and labor-market signals that the ClawWork-style evaluations are pointing toward.
