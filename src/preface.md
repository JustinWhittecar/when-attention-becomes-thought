# Preface

*Current as of: 2026-04-22.*

In 2017, a team at Google introduced the transformer, built on a single elegant mechanism: scaled dot-product self-attention. In February 2026, a team of researchers from philosophy, machine learning, linguistics, and cognitive science published a commentary in *Nature* arguing that the problem of artificial general intelligence has been solved. Nine years separate those two moments. This book traces the research that connects them, from the architectural insight that replaced recurrence with attention, through the scaling era and its emergent abilities, the alignment revolution that directed raw capability toward human intent, the reasoning breakthroughs that gave language models something resembling deliberate thought, the mechanistic interpretability work that revealed structured computation inside apparent black boxes, and the agentic systems that turned reasoning into autonomous action.

Each stage built on the last: attention enabled scale, scale revealed emergence, emergence motivated reasoning research, reasoning enabled interpretability discoveries, and interpretability findings alongside agentic demonstrations now constitute the evidence base for the AGI argument. This review synthesizes over sixty primary sources to tell that story in a way that is technically grounded but written for anyone who wants to understand how we got here, and what it means that we did.
