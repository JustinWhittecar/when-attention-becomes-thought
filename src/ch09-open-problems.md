# Open Problems and What Comes Next

> **Status: under construction.** Reading phase.

*Last updated: 2026-04-22.*

## Narrative job

Keep it honest. The fastest way to lose credibility with a technical reader is to write a chapter that reads like a press release. Name what is unsolved.

## Reading list

1. Stanford AI Index Report, latest edition.
2. Hendrycks et al., "Humanity's Last Exam," most recent iteration.
3. A recent Anthropic or OpenAI post on Responsible Scaling / Preparedness.
4. Gu & Dao (2023), Mamba. Or the latest non-transformer architecture paper worth engaging with.
5. A 2026 paper on automated interpretability.

## Themes to develop

- Reliability: hallucination, brittle generalization, the gap between benchmark performance and production utility.
- Interpretability at scale: the 25% coverage problem, and what would have to change.
- Safety and governance: the gap between what an agent can do and what it should be allowed to do.
- Post-transformer architectures: state-space models, diffusion language models, large concept models.
- The societal questions: what we owe to the systems we have built, what they owe to us, and the world we are building together.

## What to watch

Frontier model release notes and their own stated limitations. New evaluations that stress-test reliability rather than capability. Any architectural shift that displaces the transformer at the frontier.
